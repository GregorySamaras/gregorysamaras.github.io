Project

Cloud & Workplace Architecture

This work focused on designing and evolving modern workplace platforms where identity, endpoint management, cloud services, security controls, and user experience operate together as a single ecosystem. The objective was not simply to deploy technology, but to create environments that are secure, scalable, maintainable, and practical to operate.

Context

Modern workplace platforms are increasingly built around cloud services, identity-driven access controls, and centrally managed endpoints. Technologies such as Azure Virtual Desktop, Windows 365, Microsoft 365, Entra ID, and Intune provide flexibility and scale, but also introduce architectural decisions that affect security, governance, operational complexity, and user experience.

Several platform initiatives were taking place simultaneously, including cloud PC adoption, virtual desktop modernisation, identity transformation, and management model changes. Success depended on ensuring these areas evolved together rather than independently.

What the work involved

  • Designing and reviewing Azure Virtual Desktop and Windows 365 platform architecture.
  • Planning transitions from traditional hybrid management approaches toward modern cloud-managed models.
  • Evaluating how identity, endpoint management, compliance, and security controls should integrate into platform design.
  • Supporting architectural decisions around scalability, operational ownership, governance, and lifecycle management.
  • Reviewing existing dependencies and identifying opportunities to simplify platform operations while improving resilience and security.

What mattered most

The most important lesson was that architecture is rarely about technology alone.

Decisions involving identity, endpoint management, cloud services, compliance requirements, and user experience are closely connected. Improvements in one area can introduce complexity elsewhere if broader platform relationships are not considered.

Effective architecture creates clarity. It should simplify operations, support security objectives, and provide a platform that remains manageable as business requirements evolve.

Outcome

The work contributed to a clearer long-term direction for cloud and end-user computing platforms, helping align security, governance, identity, and operational requirements within a consistent architectural model.

It also supported ongoing modernisation initiatives by creating a stronger foundation for future platform evolution and cloud-native management approaches.
