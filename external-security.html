Project

External Security Exposure

This work focused on understanding externally visible security findings, identifying ownership, and helping translate technical observations into practical remediation activities. It involved investigation, analysis, and coordination rather than simply applying technical fixes.

Context

External security monitoring was identifying TLS issues, certificate findings, and configuration weaknesses across multiple environments.

The challenge was rarely the finding itself. Understanding what service was affected, who owned it, and how remediation could realistically be delivered often required additional investigation.

What the work involved

  • Reviewing externally reported findings.
  • Validating whether issues were genuine, contextual, or already understood.
  • Mapping findings back to infrastructure, services, and ownership.
  • Separating clearly actionable items from findings that needed more context.
  • Supporting remediation discussions around prioritisation, feasibility, and risk.

What mattered most

The most important part was turning fragmented findings into something actionable.

Technical findings only become useful when ownership, context, and remediation paths are understood. In many cases, the investigation and coordination effort was more valuable than the technical observation itself.

This reinforced the importance of visibility, accountability, and clear communication when addressing security issues that span multiple teams, platforms, or environments.

Outcome

The work helped improve understanding of externally exposed risks, clarify ownership of findings, and support a more structured approach to remediation and prioritisation.

It also helped strengthen visibility into how externally reachable services were configured and managed over time.
