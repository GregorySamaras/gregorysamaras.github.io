Project

Identity & Access Controls

This work focused on improving how access is designed, governed, and maintained across hybrid and cloud environments. The aim was to make identity controls more consistent, risk-aware, and practical for real users and systems.

Context

Identity sits at the centre of many modern security decisions. Access to applications, platforms, data, and devices increasingly depends on whether identity, device state, and policy are aligned properly.

The challenge was not only configuring access controls, but understanding how they affected users, devices, cloud services, and legacy environments that were still part of the organisation.

What the work involved

  • Designing and refining Conditional Access policies across Entra ID.
  • Supporting SAML SSO integrations for enterprise applications.
  • Reviewing access models with a focus on lifecycle risk, least privilege, and auditability.
  • Rationalising legacy GPO and access-related controls where they overlapped with modern identity management.
  • Considering how identity, device compliance, and access decisions interact across AVD, Windows 365, and Microsoft 365 services.

What mattered most

The most important part was making access control understandable and sustainable.

Identity controls can become fragile when policies are created reactively or without a clear model. The goal was to reduce ambiguity, avoid unnecessary complexity, and make access decisions easier to explain, maintain, and review.

This work also reinforced how closely identity is tied to endpoint state, application ownership, user lifecycle, and business process. Access control is not just a security setting; it is part of how the organisation operates.

Outcome

The work helped create a more consistent approach to identity and access control, improving alignment between users, devices, applications, and security policy.

It also supported better audit readiness and reduced reliance on legacy or unclear access patterns by moving more decisions into modern, identity-driven controls.
